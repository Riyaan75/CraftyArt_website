Phulkari

Phulkari is the traditional hand embroidery work of Punjab. Phulkari means 'flower craft'. It is very vibrant and attractive. As per famous historians say that it originated in Punjab in the 15th century. The word "Phulkari" first time appeared in Punjabi folklore "Heer Ranjha", which was written by famous poet "Waris Shah" in 17th century. In the earlier times, Phulkari was hand crafted on dupattas by the women, and these were gifted to their daughters on the occasion of their wedding. So there were so many emotions attached to this piece of priceless artwork. With the passage of time, commercialization of Phulkari started and artisans started making these beautiful pieces of work on Khaddar fabric which were called baghs. But now a days, There is a lots of variety of fabrics on which Phulkari is made like Chinon, Chiffon, Half Pure, Pure Chiffon, Cotton, Silk, Kota net and Chanderi. Products being made with Phulkari work are Phulkari Dupatta, Punjabi Suit, Phulkari Saree, Patiala Salwar, Phulkari Skirt, Phulkari Chunni, Phulkari for Bride and wedding occasions. Phulkari is also being done for home decoration and accessories, for example Phulkari Bed sheets, Phulkari dining Table runners, Phulkari Cushion Covers, Phulkari potlis, Phulkari Clutches. TILEDAR JUTTIS

Punjabi Juttis (shod) are traditional footwear of Punjab. Tiledar juttis are finely embroidered with Zari and Gold threads. Both men and women wear these. These juttis are made in Patiala where the Mochi (cobbler) Jinngar community of Rajasthan, who have now been settled in Punjab for several generations make this footwear. Earlier they used to make horse saddles and reigns (ghore ka jinn) which were sent to places like Lahore. The juttis were made in a wide area, from Udaipur to Hindumal Kot in Rajasthan. The artisans who make these footwear in Jalalabad region of Fazilka migrated from Kasur now in Pakistan after 1947. The craft of making juttis has been practiced in Patiala for the last 300 years by Raijer Mochis, who migrated from Sikal and Shikawat in Rajasthan. Jutti making has been a family tradition for many years.

SARKANDA BASKETS

Punjab Art and Craft includes the fascinating and indigenous work of Basketry. Some of the Basketry works of Punjab is known all over the world. One of the famous baskets of Punjab is Sarkanda baskets that made with Sarkanda grass. In winters, this grass dries up and after shaving the workers turns them into beautiful baskets. Grace your home with these beautiful Sarkanda items. Visit the haat bazaar of Gobindgarh Fort and witness the beauty of this beautiful handcrafted baskets.
